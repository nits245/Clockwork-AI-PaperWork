## COORDINATOR ACCESS RIGHTS (REF-007)

### When Rights Apply
Premises Coordinator access rights are activated when ANY trial participant, program participant, or team member occupies the room, regardless of other occupant types.

### Access Permissions
**Business Hours (9am-6pm, excluding quiet hours):**
- Entry for safety inspections and emergency response
- Program delivery and skills training sessions
- Participant setup and room transition activities
- Maintenance and equipment servicing
- Standards compliance and quality assurance
- Notice: Knock and announce before entry

**Outside Business Hours:**
- Emergency situations only
- 3 knocks over 2.5 minutes before entry
- Non-emergency access requires advance arrangement

### Personal Area Protection (REF-006)
**Bed and Storage Areas:**
- 72-hour advance notice required for any changes
- Written consent required for moving or rearranging personal belongings
- Personal belongings receive careful handling and temporary storage
- Documentation of changes with before/after photos

**Flexible Bed Configuration Rules (REF-006):**
- Flexible beds always configured as single beds in shared rooms to prevent jealousy
- Exception: Couples or established bed-sharing partnerships may use double configuration when both parties share same room
- Coordinator approval and roommate consent required for any double bed usage

### Program Activity Integration
**Room Usage During Business Hours:**
- Space may be needed for program delivery and training
- Participant assessments and skill development sessions
- New participant orientation and room preparation
- Collaborative learning activities and workshops
- Equipment setup for educational purposes

## TENANT RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS

### Standard Tenancy Rights
**During Quiet Hours:** Full tenant rights apply - no coordinator access except genuine emergencies  
**Personal Property:** Protection of personal belongings and designated storage areas  
**Reasonable Notice:** 72-hour minimum for any personal area changes  
**Privacy Protection:** Bathroom and personal hygiene activities remain private  
**Dispute Resolution:** Standard tenancy law remedies available

### Mixed Occupancy Considerations
**Room Sharing Dynamics:**
- All occupants must comply with house rules regardless of status
- Collaborative approach to room usage and scheduling
- Respect for different participation levels and needs
- Conflict resolution support available through management

**Notification Requirements:**
- Door notice posted indicating coordinator access rights
- Advance notice of program activities affecting room usage
- Communication channels for concerns or conflicts
- Regular check-ins regarding accommodation satisfaction

## ROOM ASSIGNMENT POLICY (REF-016)

### Safety-First Approach
Room assignments prioritize safety through:
- Same-gender groupings as default starting point
- Age-appropriate combinations where relevant
- Compatibility assessment for harmonious living
- Special needs accommodation and accessibility
- Substance abuse history consideration and support planning
- Previous incident review and risk mitigation
- Personal safety requests and protective measures

### Flexible Arrangements
**Full Occupancy Consultation:** When premises reach capacity, all residents may collaboratively determine room arrangements  
**Mutual Agreement Required:** Changes only proceed with consent from all affected parties  
**House Rules Maintained:** Commercial operations and basic standards remain non-negotiable  
**Safety Override:** Management retains final decision-making for safety concerns
